THE MURDER THAT NEVER WAS

Act Three

Characters

Muriel

Jane

Michael

Alex

The Police Inspector

Five people are gathered together in an old, creaky, country house.

Inspector: I would like you all to sit down please because I would like to discuss with you what I think is happening here.

Alex: Well that would be a relief.

Jane: I am frightened. I want out of here.

Inspector: I may not be able to help with that but hopefully I can give you an explanation. I am not sure you will be very pleased with it though. I want to ask you some questions but first We are going to talk once more about the wine glass.

Michael: Oh no!

Inspector: Oh yes, I am afraid as it is more important that you appreciate.

Jane: Well I am glad there is someone that doesn't think I am crazy.

Inspector: And also the whiskey glass?

Jane: Oh you know about that? You were upstairs when we discussed it.

Inspector: Yes I was, but still I know about it.

Muriel: That's a bit weird

Alex: Everything seems a bit weird if you ask me.

Inspector: Yes it really does.

Michael: And getting weirder by the minute I think.

Inspector: Prepare yourself sir for what you will probably find even weirder.

Jane: Is that possible.

Inspector: If my suspicions are right, definitely. Jane, you first noticed the oddity with the wine glass, what were your thoughts about that?

Jane: Seems silly really but I kind of felt someone was playing about with us, trying to confuse us.

Inspector: I see.

Jane: I even thought it might be you.

Inspector: No, it was definitely not me. So has anybody else noticed anything odd about this situation?

Muriel: What do you mean odd?

Inspector: Just anything that may seem unusual?

Alex: I can't say that I have.

Inspector. You may remember before I did the search that some of you seemed to think I was being very slow and not really doing my job.

Michael: I didn't mean to be rude but it did seem that way.

Inspector: I was observing. Observing and thinking.

Michael: Ok that's fair enough, and what did you observe?

Inspector: Similar observations to those made by Jane. She seems to be the only one that has actually questioned seriously what is going on.

Muriel: So what is going on inspector?

Inspector: I asked you all if you had noticed anything unusual. Apart from Jane, none of you had.

Alex: That's true.

Inspector: What about the lights?

Michael: What lights?

Inspector: The room lights Michael.

Michael: There are no room lights inspector.

Inspector: Exactly. Do you not think it curious that we have been here all this time in virtual darkness?

Muriel: Oh my gosh! Yes. How come nobody noticed?

Alex: I guess it just got dark after we arrived and we just didn't get around to putting the lights on.

Inspector: That is one explanation of course.

Muriel: No. No, it isn't. It has been dark all the time we have been here.

Jane: This is getting weirder and weirder. I thought somebody was messing with us now it looks like we are messing with ourselves.

Inspector: Would you say it appears that we are a little like a jigsaw puzzle? The pieces are there but not quite fit together yet?

Jane: Yes! That is a good description, that is exactly how it feels.

Murial: This is really frightening, what on Earth is happening?

Inspector: Let me ask you all something else?

Muriel: Is this going to be even more frightening?

Inspector: Even if it is, I cannot see how to avoid it, if we want the truth

. Alex: I definitely want the truth.

Inspector. Very well then. Who called me?

Jane: What do you mean?

Inspector: I am here to investigative a murder, it seems..... Lets assume I was not just walking about knocking on doors saying any murders here. Somebody must have called me. Called the police. So who did that?

(there is a moments silence)

Jane: I didn't call.

Muriel: Nor did I

Michael: Who the heck did then, because I didn't.

Inspector: You see, one more thing that you didn't observe.

Muriel: I am glad I am sitting down. My knees feel weak.

Alex: I must say Inspector, you are really beginning to shock me.

Michael: Me too. I am utterly, utterly confused now.

Inspector: It is giving me no pleasure to confuse you but I did want to give you some answers.

Jane: I don't feel we have any answers yet.

Inspector: Possibly not.

Alex: I would definitely like some.

Inspector: Michael, you seemed a little confused about the glass of whisky. So let me ask you something.

Michael: Ok.

Inspector: How did you arrive here?

Michael: What do you mean?

Inspector: A simple question. How did you get here? Bus? Train? Did you drive?

(there is a short silence)

Michael: Oh dear! This is really crazy, I don't know.

Inspector: The answer I expected. Do any of you know?

(another short silence)

Jane: I feel really, very frightened now.

Muriel: Me too and have this feeling that we should never have questioned anything. I feel we have broken some sort of rule.

Jane: Yes Moo it feels exactly like that.

Inspector: You feel we have opened Pandora's box?

Muriel: Yes.

Alex: Just a minute. Don't you think it is strange that you have a notion about Pandora's box but Michael cannot remember how he arrived here, and for that matter nor do I.

Inspector: Yes I agree that is very strange.

Muriel: Have we been hypnotised inspector?

Jane: That would be a likely explanation.

Alex: Or have we all been drugged to give us memory loss? You know when people have memory loss some things they remember but some things they don't?

Muriel: Yes! That could be it.

Michael: But who would want to drug us? And why?

Alex: We wouldn't know would we, because we wouldn't be able to remember.

Muriel: So what has this got to do with murder, and murder weapons?

Jane: Is someone drugging us so that we will commit murder?

Michael: How abut you Inspector? You have no idea either how you got here so what is your explanation?

Inspector: I do have it in my head that I should solve a murder but as we all know, there hasn't been one.

Muriel: So if it is only us here and there is a murder, none of us actually know if we are the murderer or the victim.

Alex: And I guess you have no idea either Inspector?

Inspector: No. I am afraid at this stage, I don't.

Jane: So why don't we just leave. Walk out. Then we can't be murdered or be the murderer?

Inspector: I have not tried it but something tells me that would not be possible.

Alex: Why not?

Inspector: Just a feeling I have.

Michael: Only on way to find out, let's try the door.

Alex: Good idea.

Michael. (pause) The inspector is right. It's locked or jammed or something.

Jane: How about the window let me try.

Muriel: I 'll help

(there is a bit of grunting)

Jane: No, this is stuck too, it won't shift

Muriel: Jane, Jane. Look out of the window

Jane: What? There is nothing there.

Muriel: Exactly. Nothing, nothing at all. No street lights, no buildings, nothing. Just blank.

Jane: This is so very, very scary

Muriel: Yes I am very frightened now. How will we get out?

Alex: It really does seem like we can't

Michael: We also seem to have no idea where we actually are

Muriel: Inspector, please, what is happening?

Jane: Are we ever going to get out?

Inspector: I am almost as foxed as you are.

Alex: What do you mean? Almost?

Inspector: I have some idea but the answer is almost as frightening as the question.

Jane: Whatever it is I want to know.

Michael: We all do.

Inspector: If I explain, I feel you my not believe me anyway.

Alex: Tell us though. I want to know, whatever it is.

Michael: Me too.

Inspector: Lets look at the evidence – such as it is.

Michael: That's a start.

Inspector: Nothing makes any real sense does it?

Michael: That's true enough.

Jane: I hate to say it but yes, the glasses thing.

Inspector: Exactly. That made no sense at all.

Alex: I will admit that now.

Inspector: Good, that's a start.

Michael: Me too, you were right Jane, it was very odd.

Inspector: Then of course there are the murder weapons.

Muriel: Yes, why four of them?

Inspector: That is what I couldn't understand. There seemed no point or reason to have four of them. Unless...

Alex: Unless what inspector?

Inspector: Unless there were going to be four separate murders.

Jane: Which I suppose seems unlikely.

Inspector: And the most confusing thing of course, none of us actually remembers how we got here.

Muriel: We must have been drugged.

Inspector. No, I don't think so.

Alex: It does seem a feasible explanation.

Inspector: Maybe, but I think it is far more sinister than that.

Jane: Oh my goodness.

Inspector: And of course I was here to investigate a murder, but none of you actually called the police.

Jane: That is true and I should tell you that there is something else I just noticed. There is no telephone.

Inspector: I also noticed that.

Michael: That is not so much a mystery, hardly anyone uses a house phone now. People just use cell phones.

Inspector: Do you have one Michael?

Michael: Yes, here in my jacket.

Inspector:Suppose I asked you to call one of your friends. Who would you call?

Michael: Well, I'd probably call ( a short silence) I... I don't know. This is so ridiculous. I cannot remember the name of even one of my friends.

Inspector: I thought that may be the case actually.

Alex:This is so crazy. What on Earth is happening?

Inspector: Check your phone Michael, make a call, any call.

Michael: Ok, and of course I will have a list of my friends on it. I can call one. Maybe someone can get us out.

Inspector: Try it.

Michael: (after another short pause) It's not working?

Alex: Not at all?

Michael: No. I mean it is more than not working. It's like one of those dummy phones that you get in phone retailers. Completely dead!

Inspector: If anyone else has a cell phone you can try it but I think you will find it the same.

Alex: I just did. And it is the same.

Inspector: So one more thing that does not make sense.

Michael: And the doors and windows. All blocked.

Inspector: Precisely.

Alex: It does seem like we are imprisoned

Jane: Yes it does.

Inspector: However, I don't think we are. Not as such.

Alex: Then what?

Inspector: At the risk of making this even more confusing, may I ask you a few more questions?

Michael: I am past trying to make any sense of this now so go ahead.

Inspector: We saw that Michael cannot remember the name of one friend. Can anyone (a short silence) I thought so. Jane, what is your Job?

Jane: Oh I can answer that I am a nurse.

Inspector: Good. Where?

Jane: In a hospital of course.

Inspector: Which one. What is its name?

Jane: (almost sobbing) I don't know.

Inspector: And I have no idea of the name of the police station that I am from. I would assume we all have little notion of what we do or where we do it.

Muriel: (loudly) The lights! The lights are back on

Alex: How? How on Earth did that happen?

Michael: How did that happen? Nobody moved.

Inspector: (exasperated)That is it!

Jane: What inspector? What is it?

Inspector: It is the final piece of evidence

Muriel: What evidence inspector?

Inspector: The light. It has confirmed my suspicions. This is going to be such a huge shock to you all.

Jane: Tell us what is it?

Inspector: We don't exist. None of us.

Jane, Alex, Muriel, Michael:(Together) WHAT!!

Inspector: Believe me, we do not actually exist.

Michael: What are you talking about, of course we exist, we can all see each other.

Inspector: Only on paper.

Alex: what do you mean? Only on paper?

Inspector: Exactly that. Its why we felt we were a little like a jigsaw puzzle being put together.

Jane: I still don't understand.

Inspector:On paper. We only exist on paper.

Muriel: What paper?

Inspector: We are just characters.

Alex: Characters?

Inspector: Yes! Only that. It is why we have no memory. We have no memory because we have no real life. We only have a meomry that has been given to us. And the confusion with the murder weapons and glasses is because there is no definite story yet.

Muriel: I don't understand. What do you mean.

Inspector: Why do you think the light just came on?

Muriel: I have no idea.

Inspector: It was the writer.

Michael: The writer?

Inspector: Yes the writer. He just came back into the room and turned on the light. We are not real. We are just characters in a story. He came back to write more. ( wistfully and slowly)Now we will know who the murderer is and who will get murdered!!!

THE END
